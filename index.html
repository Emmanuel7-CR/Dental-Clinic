FAQ

Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know before your first visit.

Do you accept NHS patients?
Yes, we accept both NHS and private patients. Our reception team can advise you on NHS charges (Bands 1–3) and help you understand what's covered. Private treatments offer more flexibility and a wider range of cosmetic options.
What should I expect on my first visit?
Your first appointment is a comprehensive consultation. We'll take digital X-rays, carry out a full oral health assessment, discuss your concerns and goals, and create a personalised treatment plan. It usually takes about 45–60 minutes. Please arrive 10 minutes early to complete your medical history form.
Is teeth whitening safe?
Absolutely. When carried out by a registered dental professional, teeth whitening is completely safe. We use clinically proven products at dentist-approved concentrations. Some patients experience mild, temporary sensitivity, which we can manage with desensitising treatments. Results typically last 12–18 months.
How long do dental implants last?
With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime. The titanium implant itself is permanent; the crown on top typically lasts 15–25 years. Good oral hygiene and regular dental check-ups are key to maximising the lifespan of your implant.
Do you offer payment plans?
Yes. We partner with leading dental finance providers to offer 0% interest-free credit for treatments over £300. Plans typically run from 6 to 24 months. Ask our reception team for details — we'll find a plan that fits your budget.
What if I'm nervous about treatment?
Dental anxiety is incredibly common, and our entire team is trained in anxiety management techniques. We work at your pace, explain every step before we do it, and offer sedation options for nervous patients. Many of our most grateful patients were previously too anxious to visit a dentist at all.
How do I cancel or reschedule an appointment?
We ask for at least 24 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling. You can do this by phone, email, or through our online patient portal. Late cancellations may incur a small admin fee. We completely understand that plans change — we'll always do our best to find you an alternative slot.
